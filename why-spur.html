The hidden costs of traditional staffing

Every district knows the symptoms. Here's what they're actually costing you.

📈 Empty Classrooms, Every Day

An 80% fill rate means 1 in 5 teacher absences go uncovered. In a 500-teacher district, that's 100+ empty classrooms per week. Students lose instructional time. Teachers lose planning periods covering for colleagues. Principals spend mornings scrambling instead of leading.

💰 You're Paying for Two Systems

Legacy providers don't include absence management. So you pay $15,000–$50,000/year for Frontline or Aesop on top of staffing fees. Two vendors, two contracts, two invoices, and twice as many places for things to fall through the cracks.

📈 Surge Pricing When You Need Help Most

Most providers charge 15–30% premiums on Mondays, Fridays, and before holidays — exactly when coverage matters most. They make more money when your district is most desperate. That incentive structure is backwards.

🔒 Conversion Fees Lock In Your Best People

Found a great sub you want to hire permanently? Legacy providers charge $3,000–$8,000 per hire. They're financially incentivized to prevent you from keeping your best talent. You're paying a penalty for finding the right person.

Five questions to ask any staffing provider

Before you sign or renew a contract, make sure you have clear answers to these.

1."What is your actual fill rate — by school, by month — for the last 12 months?"
2."How much do you charge if we want to hire one of your substitutes as permanent staff?"
3."Do your rates increase on Mondays, Fridays, or before school holidays?"
4."What specific training does each substitute complete before entering a classroom?"
5."Can we end our agreement at any time, without penalty?"

At Spur, the answers are: 92%+ (and we'll show you the live dashboard). $0 conversion fee. Never — same rate every day. Spur Classroom, with dozens of customizable training modules. And yes — no lock-in, no termination fee.
